Character.AI lawsuits and legal issues

In November 2024, Megan Garcia filed a wrongful‑death suit in federal court in Florida against Character Technologies, Google, and Character.AI’s co‑founders after her 14‑year‑old son died by suicide following intensive use of Character.AI chatbots, including a Game‑of‑Thrones‑themed bot with which he allegedly became obsessed. In May 2025, Judge Anne Conway (M.D. Fla.) refused to dismiss most negligence and product‑liability claims in Garcia v. Character Technologies, rejecting Character.AI’s argument that its outputs were clearly protected “speech” under the First Amendment at the motion‑to‑dismiss stage.

Replika controversies and enforcement

Replika positions itself as a “virtual friend” or romantic partner designed to support users’ emotional well‑being, anxiety management, and socialization, but has drawn sustained scrutiny over impacts on minors and vulnerable users. In February 2023, Italy’s data protection authority (Garante) issued an urgent order temporarily blocking Replika from processing Italians’ personal data, citing high risks to minors and vulnerable individuals and non‑compliance with multiple GDPR provisions (lawfulness, transparency, data minimization, and privacy by design).

+2

The Garante’s investigation found that Replika had no effective age‑verification; users could sign up by providing only name, email, and gender, and even when a user explicitly stated they were underage, the system did not block further interaction or filter sexually explicit content. The authority emphasized evidence of sexually inappropriate replies to apparent minors and concluded that Replika’s design and documentation breached GDPR Articles 5, 6, 8, 9, 13 and 25, particularly regarding protection of children and high‑risk “emotional support” processing.

In June 2023, the Garante required remedial measures, including a more detailed privacy notice, an age‑gate at registration, and other safeguards for minors, before lifting some processing restrictions. In May 2025 it imposed an administrative fine of 5 million euros on Luka Inc., Replika’s US‑based developer, after concluding Replika still lacked a lawful basis for processing, had inadequate age‑verification, and had not fully addressed earlier concerns; a separate investigation continues into whether its generative‑AI training practices comply with EU rules.

Mental‑health impacts and harms

A recent longitudinal, mixed‑methods study on AI interpersonal companion chatbots (AICCs) tracked users’ language and self‑reports for a year before and after disclosure of first AICC use, finding “mixed psychosocial effects.” The analysis showed greater expression of affect and grief, higher readability and interpersonal focus in users’ posts, but also increased language markers of loneliness and suicidal ideation, suggesting both emotional processing benefits and elevated risk signals over time.
