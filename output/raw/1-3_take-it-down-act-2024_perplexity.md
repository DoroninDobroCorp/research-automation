The TAKE IT DOWN Act is a 2025 federal law (introduced in 2024) that criminalizes non‑consensual intimate imagery (NCII), including deepfakes, and requires “covered platforms” to remove such content within 48 hours of a valid notice, with FTC enforcement of platform duties.

Overview and scope

The law’s formal name is the “Tools to Address Known Exploitation by Immobilizing Technological Deepfakes on Websites and Networks Act,” commonly called the TAKE IT DOWN Act.

48‑hour removal clock

Once a platform receives a valid notice from the identifiable individual (or authorized representative):
aitogether
+4

It must remove the reported NCII “as soon as possible, but not later than 48 hours” after receipt.

It must take reasonable efforts to remove “any identical copies” it hosts or makes available (e.g., duplicate uploads, mirrors, re‑shares using the same file hash).

The duty applies regardless of whether the original posting was by a user or through some other mechanism, so long as the content is NCII and the platform is covered.

The Act provides a safe harbor for platforms that, acting in good faith, remove content they reasonably believe to be NCII pursuant to a notice, even if it is later determined not to violate the Act, which is intended to reduce hesitation to act quickly.
